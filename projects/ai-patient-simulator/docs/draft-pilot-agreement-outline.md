### 1. Purpose and scope

1.1 This agreement governs the conduct of a formative educational pilot (the "Pilot")
    of the [Platform Name] AI patient simulator platform (the "Platform") at the College,
    commencing on approximately [date] and concluding on approximately [date].

1.2 The Pilot is limited to [number] course(s) and an estimated maximum of [number]
    students. Any expansion of scope, institution, or student cohort requires a written
    amendment.

1.3 This agreement is a pilot cooperation agreement only. It does not create an employment,
    agency, joint-venture, or partnership relationship between the parties.

---

### 2. Confidentiality

2.1 Each party agrees to keep confidential: (a) the non-public technical details and design
    of the Platform (Platform Operator's confidential information); (b) student performance
    data and any information about student participation in the Pilot (College's confidential
    information and personal data subject to its own obligations); (c) the commercial terms
    of this agreement and any related commercial discussions between the parties.

2.2 Neither party may disclose the other party's confidential information to any third party
    without prior written consent, except as required by law or professional obligation.

2.3 The Platform Operator may refer to Gome Gevim College as a pilot partner in internal
    materials and, with the College's prior written consent, in external communications.
    No public announcement may be made without the College's express written consent.

[CONFIDENT POSITION: standard bilateral confidentiality clause, appropriate for this
relationship. No special drafting concerns under Israeli law.]

---

### 3. Handle-to-student mapping: division of responsibility

3.1 Under this Pilot, the Platform will operate on a pseudonymous-handle model. The Platform
    will NOT collect or store the real names, identity numbers, email addresses, or any
    identifying credentials of individual students.

3.2 Each participating student will be assigned an opaque handle (e.g., "Student-7F2A")
    by the designated lecturer. The Platform will process session data against the handle
    only.

3.3 The College -- specifically, the designated lecturer(s) -- will maintain the mapping
    between each student's handle and their real identity ("Handle Mapping") in the
    College's own records (offline or in the College's own systems). The Handle Mapping
    will NOT be transmitted to or stored in the Platform.

3.4 The parties acknowledge that:
    (a) The Platform Operator is not a data processor of student personal data under
        Israeli Privacy Protection Law (PPL) for the Pilot, because the Platform does not
        hold or process information from which a student can be identified by the Platform
        Operator.
    (b) The College holds the Handle Mapping and is responsible for its own data-governance
        obligations in respect of that mapping under Israeli PPL, Ministry of Education
        guidelines, and any applicable institutional data policy.
    (c) If a student exercises a data-subject right (access, correction, or deletion) in
        respect of their Pilot data, the College will communicate the relevant handle to
        the Platform Operator, who will then action the request in respect of data held
        against that handle.

3.5 The College warrants that it has authority to enrol the participating students in this
    Pilot and to operate the Handle Mapping as described in clause 3.3.

[NOTE TO OWNER: clauses 3.3-3.5 are the most important legal clauses in this document.
They establish the pseudonymous boundary and document that the college -- not the platform
operator -- holds the re-identification key. This is what makes Option A legally coherent.
Adam / the College must understand and agree to this before the Pilot starts.]

[FLAGGED UNCERTAIN: whether the College's own institutional data policies or Ministry of

### 4. Data use -- limited to the Pilot

4.1 The Platform Operator will use student session data (stored against pseudonymous
    handles) only for: (a) delivering the simulation sessions to participating students;
    (b) providing formative feedback to students and their lecturers; (c) internal platform
    development and improvement during the Pilot period, using aggregated and non-identified
    data only.

4.2 The Platform Operator will NOT use student session data or performance records for:
    (a) training or fine-tuning any AI or machine learning model;
    (b) any commercial purpose unrelated to the Pilot;
    (c) any purpose involving identification of individual students.

4.3 On termination of this agreement (clause 7), the Platform Operator will delete or
    anonymise all Pilot data within [30] days, unless a longer period is agreed in writing
    for specific legitimate purposes (e.g., academic publication with full anonymisation).

4.4 The College may request a copy of aggregated, non-identified Pilot outcome data for
    its own internal academic review purposes. The Platform Operator will provide this
    in a reasonable format on written request.

### 6. No warranty / pilot nature

6.1 The Platform is provided for pilot purposes. The Platform Operator does not warrant
    that the Platform will be error-free or uninterrupted.

6.2 The Platform Operator does not warrant that AI-generated patient simulation content
    is clinically accurate or appropriate for any diagnostic or treatment purpose. The
    Platform is an educational learning tool only.

6.3 The College is responsible for ensuring that the Pilot is used appropriately within
    its own academic and professional framework.

6.4 The liability of the Platform Operator to the College under this agreement is limited
    to direct damages arising from material breach, and is capped at [ILS amount or zero
    for a no-fee pilot]. Neither party is liable for indirect, consequential, or
    reputational loss.

[NOTE TO OWNER: the liability cap figure should be discussed with the owner. For a
zero-fee pilot, a zero-cap or nominal-cap is typical. Eyal recommends keeping it simple
and low for a formative pilot.]

### 7. Term and termination

7.1 This agreement commences on the date of the last signature and continues until the
    end of the Pilot period (clause 1.1), unless earlier terminated.

7.2 Either party may terminate this agreement with [14 days] written notice.

7.3 Either party may terminate immediately on written notice if the other party commits
    a material breach that is not remedied within [7 days] of written notice of the breach.

7.4 On termination: (a) the College will cease using the Platform; (b) the Platform
    Operator will delete or anonymise all Pilot data within [30] days (clause 4.3).

---

### 8. Governing law and jurisdiction

This agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Israel. Any dispute arising from
this agreement will be referred to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of [Tel Aviv
or as agreed].

---

### 9. Entire agreement

This agreement (together with any written amendments) constitutes the entire agreement
between the parties in respect of the Pilot. It supersedes all prior discussions and
representations. It does not affect the separate commercial negotiations between the
parties (clause 5.3).
