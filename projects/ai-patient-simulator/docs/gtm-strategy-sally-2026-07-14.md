## 1. Target Segment Ordering

**Recommended order: (1) Israel -> (2) UK LI-CBT/PWP -> (3) US**

### Segment 1 -- Israel Academic (Pilot Home Turf)

Rationale:
- Zero identified domestic competition in Hebrew or Arabic psychotherapy simulation. Not a gap
  we inferred -- no competitor in the full competitive table (direct, adjacent, or defunct)
  claims Hebrew or Arabic support. [Zvika, Section 3.1 Gap 1, HIGH confidence]
- Adam's institution is our design-partner anchor. If it is in Israel (not yet confirmed --
  see Section 7 open decision OD-1), the pilot is already a beachhead entry.
- Israeli academic procurement is relationship-driven and less formally gated than UK HE
  procurement (no mandatory tender thresholds equivalent to UK public sector rules).
  [INFERENCE based on general Israeli HE norms; no primary data found -- Zvika, Section 2.3]
- Post-Oct 2023 government priority on mental health capacity creates a demand tailwind.
  Hebrew University launched an Institute for Traumatic Stress and Recovery in 2024. [Zvika,
  Section 2.3, EVIDENCE: JTA + AFHU sources]
- Risk: Israeli market budget envelopes for training tools are unknown -- no public data found.
  [Zvika, Section 7, data gap]

### Segment 2 -- UK LI-CBT/PWP University Beachhead

Rationale:
- ~20+ UK universities deliver NHS-funded PWP and High-Intensity Talking Therapies training.
  NHS England provides structural funding for trainee throughput through at least 2037 (Long
  Term Workforce Plan target: 24,000-26,000 additional psychological professions posts).
  [Erez, Section 2.2; Zvika, Section 2.1]
- No psychotherapy-specific, LTI-1.3-native platform with competency mapping to IAPT/PWP
  frameworks exists. CBT Trainer maps only to CTS-R (CBT); no competitor maps to ACT or
  LI-CBT PWP competency scales. [Zvika, Section 3.1 Gap 2, MEDIUM confidence]
- UK institutional procurement is 3-12 months and requires a pilot period. [Erez, Section 2.4]
  We cannot close a UK university deal without a published pilot paper as the credibility anchor
  -- every competitor with traction used one (CBT Trainer: JMIR 2026; Client101: JMIR 2025 +
  BMC 2025; TMind: Simmons pilot). [Zvika, Section 4.2]
- BABCP Annual Conference is the targeted channel for UK clinical education tools. [Zvika,
  Section 4.2, INFERENCE]
- CBT Trainer at UCL is a beachhead overlap risk: target Sheffield, Reading, Southampton,
  Newcastle for first UK approaches -- not UCL. [Zvika, Section 3.2 Weakness 4]
- Erez conservative SAM: $72-120K ARR at full UK LI-CBT/PWP penetration. Small but concrete.
  [Erez, Section 2.2]

### Segment 3 -- US Social Work / Counseling Programs

Rationale for deferral:
- TMind AI has 9+ named US universities. SimCare AI has YC pedigree and $2M. The US is the
  most contested sub-segment now. [Zvika, Section 2.4]
- Enter only after Israel and UK reference cases are established. The premium-tier argument
  (LTI grade-return, rubric engine, multi-tenant governance) needs to be evidenced by
  published results before US programme directors will engage. [INFERENCE]

### Israel vs UK: Why Israel First

The design-partner pilot IS the Israel entry, assuming Adam's institution is in Israel (OD-1
below). Running the pilot there costs nothing incremental -- it is the build plan regardless.
The Israel segment then produces the peer-reviewed paper that unlocks UK institutional sales.
The two are sequential, not parallel budget commitments. UK is where ARR scales; Israel is
where credibility is built.

## 2. Pricing + Packaging Hypothesis

**HYPOTHESIS -- not adopted pricing. Erez condition 3 requires one comparable institution to
confirm WTP before building past pilot.**

### Observed benchmarks (all EVIDENCE from Zvika Section 4.1)

- SimChat (adjacent, LTI 1.3-native, general medical OSCE): GBP 10/user/year confirmed
- TMind AI (direct, individual): USD 10-50/month (USD 120-600/year) -- individual tier, not
  institutional
- CBT Trainer, SimCare AI, OMS, SimConverse: institutional pricing not disclosed

### Reconciliation

Erez hypothesis: USD 15-40/student/year for institutional SaaS. [Erez, Section 2.2 SAM math]

Zvika inference: GBP 25-50/student/year is likely supportable for a psychotherapy-specific,
LTI-1.3-native platform with competency rubrics -- a premium over SimChat GBP 10 justified by
the specificity and grade-return value. [Zvika, Section 4.1, MEDIUM confidence INFERENCE]

These are compatible in the GBP 25-40 / USD 30-50 band. My hypothesis:

**Institutional SaaS: GBP 30-40/student/year (USD 38-50) for direct programme-level contracts.**

Rationale:
- 3x SimChat on a per-user basis is defensible if we own psychotherapy competency mapping and
  LTI 1.3 grade-return that SimChat lacks in this domain
- Sits within Erez's validated range at the upper band, justified by rubric engine and
  continuing-persona feature Adam identifies as a competitive differentiator [product-signals-
  adam-2026-07-08.md]
- At GBP 35 x 40 students x 20 UK programmes = GBP 28,000 ARR at 50% UK LI-CBT/PWP
  penetration. Small but real. ASSUMPTION: 20 programmes, 40 students/programme average.

### Packaging tiers (HYPOTHESIS)

Tier 1 -- Pilot / Small Programme: flat fee GBP 3,000-8,000 for up to 50 students per year.
Reduces procurement friction below typical GBP 5K-25K tender threshold in UK HE. [Zvika,
Section 2.1 buyer note; INFERENCE on threshold range]

Tier 2 -- Institutional per-student: GBP 30-40/student/year for 50-200 student cohorts.
Annual contract; annual renewal.

Tier 3 -- Enterprise / Multi-programme: custom. For institutions running multiple therapy
programmes on a shared platform (college-programme-course hierarchy is already in the HLD).

Individual / CPD practitioners (secondary channel): USD 30-50/month. Mirrors TMind model.
Lower priority than institutional; useful for word-of-mouth among clinical educators.

### What must be validated

Erez condition 3 [viability-assessment-erez.md, Section 7.1]: one comparable institution
(outside Adam's network) must confirm WTP before committing build resources past pilot. Method:
fold this into the recruitment conversation for institution #2. When approaching 3-5 UK
programme directors with the published pilot paper, ask for budget envelope confirmation as
part of the discovery conversation. Do not present a price list; ask what they currently spend
on clinical skills tools and what they would expect to pay for on-demand simulation with LTI
grade-return.

## 3. Channel Plan

### Primary: Direct to Programme Director

Evidence pattern: every competitor with institutional traction used direct outreach to programme
managers and clinical educators as the first-sales motion. No competitor has used e-commerce or
self-serve at institutional level. [Zvika, Section 4.2]

Execution:
- Identify 3-5 UK LI-CBT/PWP programme directors at Sheffield, Reading, Southampton, Newcastle
  (avoid UCL given CBT Trainer proximity). [Erez Section 2.2; Zvika Section 2.1]
- Outreach only after the published pilot paper exists -- this is the credibility unlock. [Zvika
  Section 4.2]
- Outreach is owner-led or owner-approved. No agent contacts external parties.
- Israel: relationship-driven; Adam's network may surface warm introductions. Owner relays.

### Secondary: Academic Publication Motion (highest-leverage single action)

The "publish-before-you-sell" motion is not optional -- it is the unlock for every institutional
sale in this market. All competitors with traction have a peer-reviewed paper. We have none.
Without it, every UK sales conversation opens with a credibility deficit that CBT Trainer (JMIR
2026) has already closed. [Zvika, Section 3.2 Weakness 2]

Publication targets: JMIR Medical Education, BMC Medical Education, or Clinical Psychology
Review. Target a preprint on medRxiv/arXiv immediately post-pilot as a faster signal while
formal peer review proceeds. ASSUMPTION: co-authorship with Adam's institution is feasible and
should be negotiated into the design-partner agreement NOW.

### Tertiary: Conference / Association

BABCP Annual Conference (UK): the correct first conference appearance for the UK LI-CBT/PWP
segment. Present the pilot study findings. Typically summer each year. [Zvika, Section 4.2
and Section 2.5]

BACP (for UK counselling/psychotherapy degree programmes): the accreditation body lists dozens
of accredited providers -- a conference and/or directory listing is relevant for Segment 2
expansion (UK counselling degrees beyond NHS-funded LI-CBT/PWP). [Zvika, Section 2.2]

NOT recommended yet:
- NHS Talking Therapies OMT framework tender (022987-2025): too slow and too competitive for a
  startup without established UK institutional footprint. [Zvika, Section 2.1, INFERENCE]
- US conferences (APA, CACREP): defer until UK/Israel reference cases exist.

## 6. Risks and Countermoves

### R1: SimCare AI velocity (YC, $2M, speed)

Likelihood: MEDIUM. SimCare is US-centric with no confirmed UK or Israel footprint. [Zvika,
Section 1.1 SimCare AI -- UK/Israel presence not evidenced]

Our moat vs SimCare: Hebrew/Arabic native (unique), LI-CBT/PWP competency mapping to IAPT/BABCP
frameworks (no evidence SimCare has this), LTI 1.3 grade-return (unconfirmed for SimCare),
multi-session continuing-persona (competitive differentiator per Adam [product-signals-adam-
2026-07-08.md], not evidenced in SimCare).

Countermove: Move to UK/Israel fast. SimCare has no named UK institutional customers as of
2026-07-14. [Zvika, Section 1.1 -- "No named institutional customers disclosed publicly"] If
we have a published paper and a reference customer before SimCare secures UK institutional
traction, their YC funding cannot easily replace domain-specific clinical credibility.

### R2: SimChat adding a counselling module (highest adjacent risk)

Likelihood: MEDIUM-HIGH. SimChat is LTI 1.3-native, GBP 10/user/year, 800+ institutions, and
already lists counselling scenarios as a use case. This is the "easiest fast-follower move in
the market." [Zvika, Section 1.2 SimChat -- competitive threat level: HIGH]

Impact: If SimChat ships a basic CBT/counselling module at GBP 10/user, price competition
becomes existential for our Tier 2 institutional pricing (GBP 30-40).

Countermoves:
1. Speed: publish the pilot paper before SimChat can counter with a counselling module. The
   paper locks in the clinical credibility narrative.
2. Depth moat: SimChat adding a basic counselling scenario is not the same as IAPT/PWP
   competency mapping, CTS-R/ACT framework rubrics, dynamic emotional state engine, and
   ground-truth clinical boundaries. These are hard to copy quickly.
3. Hebrew/Arabic: SimChat has no multilingual claim. Israel is structurally protected.
4. Continuing persona / multi-session: no evidence any competitor (including SimChat) has this.
   If shipped for Sep pilot, it is a durable differentiator.

If SimChat does ship a basic counselling module at GBP 10, the countermove is to position
explicitly on clinical depth and regulatory credibility (IAPT/BABCP compliance, competency
framework mapping) rather than on price.

### R3: CBT Trainer UCL proximity

Likelihood: MEDIUM. CBT Trainer is embedded at UCL -- one of the primary LI-CBT/PWP training
institutions -- with a peer-reviewed paper already published. [Zvika, Section 3.2 Weakness 4]

Countermove: Do not target UCL. Target Sheffield, Reading, Southampton, Newcastle. Differentiate
on ACT + LI-CBT PWP protocol support (CBT Trainer is CBT-only) and on LTI 1.3 grade-return
(CBT Trainer: not confirmed). Our multi-session capability is also not evidenced in CBT Trainer.

### R4: Company registration gap (T-0034 cancelled)

No contract, no invoice, no legally executable design-partner agreement until T-0034 is
resolved or a legal workaround is found. [Task brief, open dependency]

Impact: HIGH -- this blocks the entire pilot-to-paid path at Phase 1 (contract signing).

Countermove: Owner must resolve T-0034 as the single highest-urgency non-product action. Eyal
should advise on interim structures (e.g., contracting under an existing entity) if company
registration cannot be completed before the 2026-09-01 agreement target. This is an A1 decision.

### R5: No published paper yet (credibility deficit)

We enter UK institutional conversations with no peer-reviewed validation. CBT Trainer has JMIR
2026. Client101 has JMIR 2025 + BMC 2025. TMind AI has a Simmons pilot. [Zvika, Section 3.2
Weakness 2]

Countermove: The solution is the pilot study itself. The paper is not a nice-to-have -- it is
the minimum credibility artifact for institutional clinical simulation sales. [Zvika, Section 4.2]
Co-authorship clause in the design-partner agreement is the structural fix.

### R6: Erez condition 3 unvalidated (pricing risk)

We have not confirmed WTP at any institution outside Adam's. Building a full commercial product
without this signal risks pricing at a point the market will not pay. [Erez, Section 7.1
Condition 3]

Countermove: validate in the recruitment process for institution #2. Ask budget envelope
questions before presenting pricing.

### R7: Clinical accuracy / AI safety risk

If the AI patient generates unsafe or factually wrong clinical content in a live training
session, reputational and regulatory damage in a clinical education context would be severe.
[Erez, Section 8 R3]

Countermove: ground-truth boundary engine (HLD architecture); clinical advisor sign-off on all
simulation cases before deployment (APS-010, still open per product-signals-adam-2026-07-08.md
-- clinical advisor appointment is outstanding); Adam reviews all cases before assigning to
students.
