Subject: AI Patient Simulator -- a few questions to lock the September pilot scope

Hi Adam,

Thanks again for the materials -- they gave us a clear picture, and we are now planning the
September pilot in earnest. A handful of open points need your input to keep the timeline on
track. None require anything heavy on your side beyond a reply.

1. Session format
For the September pilot specifically: should each student run a single clinical encounter per
session, or multiple sessions with the same simulated patient over time? This is the single
decision that most shapes the build, so settling it early helps us the most.

2. Clinical oversight
We want the simulation content to be clinically sound, and we would value your view on how you
see the clinical-expert role. A few specifics would help: who you think should validate the
clinical content, how involved that person would need to be, and whether you have someone in
mind. We are open on the shape -- a named advisor, periodic review, or something lighter -- and
your perspective will shape how we set it up.

3. Student welfare contact
If a student becomes distressed during a simulation, we want to show a real support resource in
the interface. Can you confirm the counseling or welfare contact appropriate for your students
to reach -- one they can actually use?

4. Cohort size
Roughly how many students and teachers do you expect in the September cohort, and about how many
sessions per student over the course? Even a broad range lets us size the setup correctly.

5. Data handling with the college
So we can put the right data-protection arrangement in place before any student uses the system:
who is the data controller for the student records -- you, the college, or us? Do you already
have a data or privacy agreement with Gome Gevim that we should align to, and does the college
require student data to be stored inside Israel?

These points are on our critical path for a clean September start, so anything you can share by
mid-July would be a big help. Happy to jump on a short call if any of them are easier to talk
through.
