## 1. Definitions

1.1 "Personal Data" means any data relating to an identified or identifiable natural
person, as defined under the Israeli Privacy Protection Law 5741-1981, as amended by
Amendment 13 ("PPL"), and, where applicable, the EU General Data Protection Regulation
2016/679 ("GDPR").

1.2 "Processing" means any operation performed on Personal Data, including collection,
recording, organization, storage, retrieval, use, disclosure, transmission, combination,
blocking, erasure, or destruction, whether by automated or manual means.

1.3 "Data Subject" means the natural person to whom Personal Data relates.

1.4 "Sub-Processor" means any third party engaged by Eco-Synthetic to carry out
Processing activities on behalf of the Controller.

1.5 "Service Agreement" means the main agreement between the Parties governing
Eco-Synthetic's delivery of the AI-assisted delivery management SaaS service
("Service").

1.6 "Privacy Protection Authority" or "PPA" means the Israeli authority established
under the PPL responsible for supervising data protection compliance.

1.7 "Security Incident" or "Breach" means any unauthorized access, use, disclosure,
modification, or destruction of Personal Data, or any breach of the security measures
applicable to it.

1.8 "Amendment 13" means Amendment 13 to the Privacy Protection Law 5741-1981,
enacted 2023 and entering into force 2025.

## 2. Subject Matter and Duration of Processing

2.1 **Subject matter.** This DPA governs the Processing of Personal Data by Eco-Synthetic
in connection with the provision of the Service to the Controller as described in the
Service Agreement. The Service consists of AI-assisted delivery management SaaS,
including scheduling, routing, customer communication workflows, and related analytics
and reporting functions.

2.2 **Duration.** Eco-Synthetic will Process Personal Data for the duration of the
Service Agreement, and thereafter only as required by applicable law or as expressly
instructed by the Controller in writing. On termination or expiry of the Service
Agreement, Eco-Synthetic will, at the Controller's election, return or delete all
Personal Data as described in Section 10 below, subject to any mandatory retention
obligations imposed by Israeli law.

---

## 3. Nature and Purpose of Processing

3.1 **Nature.** Eco-Synthetic processes Personal Data as a data processor acting on
the documented instructions of the Controller. Processing activities include: ingestion
of delivery and customer data provided by the Controller; AI-assisted scheduling,
routing, and communication workflow generation; storage of Personal Data for the
duration of the Service; generation of analytics and reports; and transmission of
processed outputs back to the Controller or its designated recipients.

3.2 **Purpose.** Processing is carried out solely for the purpose of providing the
Service to the Controller as defined in the Service Agreement. Eco-Synthetic shall
not use Personal Data for any other purpose, including training of AI models, unless
explicitly authorized in writing by the Controller and, where required, the Data Subject.

3.3 **Instruction basis.** This DPA, together with the Service Agreement, constitutes
the Controller's documented instruction to Eco-Synthetic to Process Personal Data for
the purposes stated in Section 3.2. Any material change to the instruction must be
agreed in writing between the Parties.

## 4. Categories of Personal Data and Data Subjects

4.1 **Categories of Personal Data.** The Personal Data processed under this Agreement
may include, depending on the Controller's use of the Service:

(a) Contact and identification data: names, addresses, email addresses, telephone

(recipients, drivers, customers, and contact persons);

(b) Operational data: delivery addresses, scheduling data, route data, timestamps,

(c) Communication data: messages and communication records generated through the
Service that relate to identifiable individuals;

(d) Any other categories of Personal Data the Controller chooses to input into the
Service, as disclosed to Eco-Synthetic in writing prior to processing.

## 5. Obligations of Eco-Synthetic (Processor)

5.1 **Process on instruction only.** Eco-Synthetic shall Process Personal Data only
on documented instructions from the Controller, as set out in this DPA and the Service
Agreement, unless required to do so by applicable Israeli law, in which case Eco-Synthetic
shall, to the extent permitted by law, inform the Controller before Processing.

5.2 **Confidentiality.** Eco-Synthetic shall ensure that persons authorized to Process
Personal Data are bound by an obligation of confidentiality with respect to the Personal
Data, whether under a contractual arrangement or applicable law. Eco-Synthetic shall
not disclose Personal Data to any third party except as expressly authorized by this
DPA or required by law.

5.3 **Security measures.** Eco-Synthetic shall implement and maintain appropriate
technical and organizational measures to protect Personal Data against Security Incidents,
in accordance with the requirements of Amendment 13 and the Privacy Protection
Regulations (Data Security) 5777-2017, and as further described in Section 9 of this DPA.

5.4 **Sub-processors.** Eco-Synthetic shall not engage a Sub-Processor to carry out
Processing activities on behalf of the Controller without the Controller's prior written
consent, except as set out in Section 7. Where Eco-Synthetic engages a Sub-Processor,
it shall impose data protection obligations on the Sub-Processor equivalent to those
applicable to Eco-Synthetic under this DPA. Eco-Synthetic remains fully liable to the
Controller for the performance of the Sub-Processor's obligations.

5.5 **Data Subject rights.** Eco-Synthetic shall, taking into account the nature of
the Processing, assist the Controller by appropriate technical and organizational
measures in fulfilling the Controller's obligations to respond to requests by Data
Subjects to exercise their rights under the PPL (as amended by Amendment 13), including
rights of access, correction, deletion, and objection to processing. Eco-Synthetic
shall forward any Data Subject request received directly to the Controller without
undue delay and shall not respond to such requests directly without the Controller's
prior written authorization.

5.6 **Assistance with compliance.** Eco-Synthetic shall assist the Controller in
ensuring compliance with its obligations relating to security of Processing, notification
of Security Incidents, data protection impact assessments (where applicable), and prior
consultation with the PPA, taking into account the information available to Eco-Synthetic
and the nature of the Processing.

5.7 **Deletion and return on termination.** On termination or expiry of the Service
Agreement, or upon the Controller's written request, Eco-Synthetic shall, at the
Controller's election: (a) securely delete or destroy all Personal Data and any copies
thereof; or (b) return all Personal Data to the Controller in a commonly used machine-
readable format. Eco-Synthetic shall certify such deletion or return in writing within
[PLACEHOLDER: 30] days of the Controller's request. Eco-Synthetic may retain Personal
Data to the extent required by mandatory Israeli law, in which case it shall notify the
Controller of the legal basis and scope of retention.

5.8 **Audit cooperation.** Eco-Synthetic shall, upon reasonable written notice from
the Controller (no less than [PLACEHOLDER: 14] calendar days except in the event of a
confirmed Security Incident), make available all information reasonably necessary to
demonstrate compliance with this DPA, and allow for and contribute to audits or
inspections conducted by the Controller or an auditor mandated by the Controller,
subject to: (a) confidentiality obligations applicable to Eco-Synthetic; (b) the audit
being carried out at the Controller's expense; and (c) the audit being limited in scope
and frequency to what is reasonably required for the Controller's compliance obligations.
The Parties may agree that the audit obligation is satisfied by a current third-party
certification or audit report acceptable to the Controller.

5.9 **Record of processing activities.** Eco-Synthetic shall maintain a record of
Processing activities carried out on behalf of the Controller, to the extent required
by Amendment 13 and the Privacy Protection Regulations, and shall make such records
available to the PPA upon request.

## 6. Obligations of the Controller (Customer)

6.1 **Lawful processing.** The Controller warrants and represents that it has, and
shall maintain throughout the duration of this DPA, a valid legal basis for Processing
the Personal Data under the PPL (and GDPR, if applicable), and that all instructions
given to Eco-Synthetic are lawful.

6.2 **Data accuracy.** The Controller is responsible for the accuracy, completeness,
and relevance of the Personal Data submitted to the Service.

6.3 **Data Subject notice.** The Controller is responsible for providing Data Subjects
with any required privacy notice or information regarding the Processing of their Personal
Data, including disclosure that Eco-Synthetic is engaged as a Processor.

6.4 **Authorized users.** The Controller is responsible for ensuring that only
authorized persons access the Service using the Controller's credentials, and for
managing access rights within the Controller's organization.

6.5 **Compliance obligations.** Each Party is responsible for its own compliance with
applicable data protection law with respect to its own controller or processor activities.
Nothing in this DPA relieves the Controller of its own compliance obligations as data
controller under the PPL or GDPR.

6.6 **Cooperation.** The Controller shall cooperate with Eco-Synthetic in the performance
of this DPA, including timely responding to Eco-Synthetic's requests for instructions,
and to the extent required, in making the Service compliant with any applicable regulatory
requirement.

## 8. Cross-Border Data Transfers

8.1 **Israel-EU adequacy.** Israel has been recognized by the European Commission as
providing adequate protection for personal data (Commission Decision 2011/61/EU). This
means that transfers of Personal Data from EU-based Controllers to Eco-Synthetic
(located in Israel) do not require additional transfer mechanisms under GDPR, provided
that Eco-Synthetic's processing complies with Israeli PPL as amended.

8.2 **Transfers to third countries.** Any transfer of Personal Data by Eco-Synthetic
to a Sub-Processor or other recipient located outside Israel must comply with Chapter
VIII of the PPL and Amendment 13. Where Personal Data is transferred to a country not
recognized as providing adequate protection, Eco-Synthetic shall ensure an appropriate
transfer mechanism is in place (e.g., standard contractual clauses or equivalent
safeguards under Israeli law).

8.3 **Anthropic (US).** Processing of Service data through Anthropic's API involves
transfer to the United States. The United States does not have a blanket Israeli adequacy
finding. Eco-Synthetic's obligation to ensure an appropriate transfer mechanism with
Anthropic is part of the Anthropic DPA resolution required under Section 7.1 (CONDITION).

8.4 **GDPR applicability.** If the Controller is established in the EU or EEA, or if
the Service involves processing of personal data of EU residents in the context of
offering goods or services to, or monitoring the behavior of, EU residents, GDPR may
apply in parallel with the PPL. In that case, the Parties shall cooperate in good faith
to ensure compliance with GDPR requirements applicable to each Party. [PLACEHOLDER: If
EU Controller, consider appending EU Standard Contractual Clauses (SCC) as an Annex.]

## 9. Security Measures

9.1 **Standard of protection.** Eco-Synthetic shall implement technical and organizational
security measures appropriate to the risk presented by the Processing, taking into account
the state of the art, cost, and nature, scope, context, and purposes of Processing, as
required by Amendment 13 and the Privacy Protection Regulations (Data Security) 5777-2017.

9.2 **Technical measures.** As of the Effective Date, Eco-Synthetic's technical security
measures include, as a minimum:

(a) Encryption: Personal Data is encrypted in transit (TLS 1.2 or higher) and at rest
using industry-standard encryption;

(b) Access control: access to Personal Data is restricted to authorized personnel on a
need-to-know basis; role-based access controls are maintained;

(c) Logging and monitoring: access to systems processing Personal Data is logged and
monitored for anomalies;

(d) Vulnerability management: Eco-Synthetic maintains a security review and patching
process for its software and infrastructure;

(e) [PLACEHOLDER: list additional technical measures specific to the product

9.3 **Organizational measures.** As of the Effective Date, Eco-Synthetic's organizational

(a) Confidentiality obligations: personnel with access to Personal Data are bound by

(b) Security policies: Eco-Synthetic maintains internal security policies and

(c) Incident response: Eco-Synthetic maintains a Security Incident response procedure,

9.5 **Customer environment.** Eco-Synthetic is not responsible for the security of
the Controller's own systems, networks, or the means by which the Controller accesses
the Service.

## 10. Security Incident and Breach Notification

10.1 **Notification obligation.** In the event of a confirmed Security Incident
affecting Personal Data processed under this DPA, Eco-Synthetic shall notify the
Controller without undue delay and, in any event, within 72 hours of becoming aware
of the Security Incident, in accordance with Amendment 13 to the PPL.

10.2 **Content of notification.** The breach notification shall include, to the extent
available at the time of notification:

(a) a description of the nature of the Security Incident, including where possible,
the categories and approximate number of Data Subjects and Personal Data records
affected;

(b) the name and contact details of Eco-Synthetic's point of contact for further
information;

(c) a description of the likely consequences of the Security Incident;

(d) a description of the measures taken or proposed by Eco-Synthetic to address the
Security Incident, including where appropriate, measures to mitigate its possible
adverse effects.

10.3 **Where full information is unavailable.** If Eco-Synthetic cannot provide all
information required under Section 10.2 within the initial 72-hour window, it shall
provide the information available at that time and supplement it as further information
becomes available, without undue further delay.

10.4 **Reporting to PPA.** Each Party is responsible for complying with its own
obligations to report Security Incidents to the Privacy Protection Authority under
Amendment 13. Eco-Synthetic shall cooperate with the Controller to enable the
Controller to fulfill its reporting obligations as data controller.

10.5 **Notification to Data Subjects.** If applicable law requires notification to
Data Subjects of a Security Incident, the Controller is responsible for such notification.
Eco-Synthetic shall provide reasonable assistance to the Controller in drafting or
sending such notification.

## 11. Term and Termination

11.1 **Term.** This DPA enters into force on the Effective Date and remains in force
for the duration of the Service Agreement, unless terminated earlier as provided herein
or as agreed in writing by the Parties.

11.2 **Termination for breach.** Either Party may terminate this DPA (and the Service
Agreement) immediately on written notice if the other Party commits a material breach
of this DPA and: (a) the breach is not capable of remedy; or (b) the breaching Party
fails to remedy the breach within [PLACEHOLDER: 30] calendar days of receiving written
notice specifying the breach.

11.3 **Consequences of termination.** On termination or expiry of this DPA, the
provisions of Section 5.7 (Deletion and return on termination) apply. The obligations
of Eco-Synthetic under Sections 5.2 (Confidentiality), 5.3 (Security), 9 (Security
measures), and 10 (Breach notification) shall survive termination for a period of
[PLACEHOLDER: 3 years] or for such longer period as required by applicable Israeli law.

11.4 **No waiver.** Neither Party's failure to enforce any provision of this DPA shall
constitute a waiver of that Party's right to enforce such provision subsequently.

## 12. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

12.1 **Governing law.** This DPA is governed by and construed in accordance with the
laws of the State of Israel, without regard to its conflict-of-law provisions.

12.2 **Jurisdiction.** Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this DPA shall
be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the competent courts of the Tel Aviv-Jaffa
District.

12.3 **GDPR supplement.** Where the Controller is subject to GDPR and the Parties
have agreed to incorporate EU Standard Contractual Clauses as an Annex to this DPA,
those clauses shall govern in respect of GDPR-subject processing, to the extent of
any inconsistency with this Section 12.

## 13. General Provisions

13.1 **Entire agreement.** This DPA, together with the Service Agreement and any
annexes, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the
subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, and
understandings with respect thereto.

13.2 **Amendments.** This DPA may only be amended by a written instrument signed by
authorized representatives of both Parties. Eco-Synthetic reserves the right to update
this DPA to reflect changes in applicable law or regulation; material changes will be
notified to the Controller with [PLACEHOLDER: 30] days' notice.

13.3 **Severability.** If any provision of this DPA is found to be invalid or
unenforceable by a court of competent jurisdiction, the remaining provisions shall
continue in full force and effect.

13.4 **Order of precedence.** In the event of a conflict between the terms of this
DPA and the Service Agreement with respect to data processing matters, this DPA shall
prevail to the extent of the conflict.

13.5 **Counterparts and electronic signature.** This DPA may be executed in counterparts.
An electronic signature shall be deemed valid and binding to the same extent as a
handwritten signature, to the extent permitted by Israeli law (Electronic Signature Law
5761-2001).
