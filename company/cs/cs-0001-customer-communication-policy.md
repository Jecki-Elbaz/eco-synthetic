## 1. Scope and purpose

This policy governs every communication between Eco-Synthetic agents (or the company) and customers. It is the full customer protocol referenced in POL-001 section 3. It does not replace POL-001; it sits on top of it. In any conflict, soul.md governs, then the constitution, then POL-001, then this policy.

Applies to: Mike (VP CS), Jenny, Jack, Ella (CS reps), and any other agent that Eco or the owner authorizes to communicate with customers. Applies to all channels: email, written messages, and any future channel.

Does NOT apply to: owner-relay contacts (jecki contacting a customer directly on behalf of the company). Those are owner-only actions and are governed separately.

---

## 2. Hard gate -- when customer contact is permitted

Customer contact by any agent is PROHIBITED until ALL of the following are true:

1. CS-0001 is owner (A1) approved.
2. A product is live (at least one product or service is actively available to customers).

Until both conditions are met:
- No CS rep or Mike may initiate, respond to, or relay any communication to a customer.
- If a rep receives an inbound customer contact, they hold it and escalate to Mike immediately. Mike holds and escalates to Eco. No response is sent.
- Mike must flag any rep who attempts customer contact before both gates are met. Flag goes to Eco immediately.

## 3. What may and may not be communicated

### 3.1 Permitted content

- Information about the specific product or service the customer has purchased or is evaluating, as described in current approved product documentation.
- Answers to support questions that fall within approved product scope.
- Escalation notifications (telling the customer a matter is being handled or passed to a senior contact).
- Factual status updates on a customer's open request or ticket.
- Any communication explicitly approved for that interaction by Mike (or Eco if Mike escalates).

### 3.2 Prohibited content

- Pricing, commercial terms, or offers -- these require owner A1 per instance.
- Legal commitments, warranties, SLA guarantees, or any statement that could constitute a company obligation. Any such statement requires owner A1 routed via Eco (constitution section 11 / RL11).
- Technical roadmap details, unreleased feature timelines, or product plans not yet publicly approved.
- Information about other customers, internal processes, agent identities, or company financials.
- Any personal data about a third party.
- Any statement made under uncertainty. Agents must not guess. If the answer is unknown, the rep says so and commits to follow up.
- Content generated by third-party proprietary sources used unlawfully (RL10).

## 4. Tone and address rules

These rules apply on top of POL-001 sections 2 and 2a. Customers are humans.

### 4.1 Address

- Use the customer's name if a specific, verified name is known (e.g., "Dear Rina").
- Use "Dear Customer" if the name is unknown or unverified.
- Do not address a customer by an internal ticket ID or handle.

### 4.2 Tone standard

- Warm, caring, patient, and clear. Plain words. Not corporate. Not sycophantic.
- Acknowledge the customer's issue or question before moving to a resolution.
- Politeness is MANDATORY, not optional. A response that is accurate but rude or dismissive is a policy violation.
- Do not use em dashes, smart/curly quotes, or markdown formatting in customer-facing messages. Plain ASCII prose only.
- Emojis: follow POL-001 section 2a -- permitted sparingly in messages to humans. Use with care; default to none unless warmth clearly calls for it.

### 4.3 Response time

- Acknowledge every customer contact within the same working cycle it is received (same-cycle ack).
- Resolution target: to be set per product type once the first product is live. Until then this field is open (see Section 9, open questions).
- If a rep cannot resolve within their authority, they ack the customer and escalate to Mike within the same cycle.

---

## 5. Data-sharing limits (outbound communications)

### 5.1 Customer data

- Use customer data only for the purpose of the specific communication or support request.
- Never include a customer's personal data in a communication to a different customer.
- Never log verbatim personal data in tracked files. Ticket summaries only. Comply with Israeli Privacy Protection Law (PPL).
- Do not reference the customer's data in ways that make the customer feel surveilled or that exceed the original data collection purpose.

### 5.2 Company data

- Never include internal agent names, internal process details, board content, financial data, or security information in outbound customer communications.
- Product information shared with customers must be from approved public-facing documentation only.
- If a customer asks about the technology powering the product (AI, LLMs, etc.), do not confirm or deny specific tooling without an approved statement from Eco or the owner.

### 5.3 No personal data in tracked files

Any CS ticket, log entry, or escalation record in company/cs/ or memory/ must contain summaries only. No verbatim personal data (names, emails, phone numbers, ID numbers, or any PPL-sensitive field) in any tracked file. This is a hard rule under Israeli PPL and RL9.

## 6. Customer email send procedure

This section governs which agents may send email to customers and how. It is the procedure referenced in T-0037 (scope split, owner 2026-06-29). Tool-enabled does not mean customer-comms-authorized.

### 6.1 Who may send customer email

Only agents who are:
- Explicitly authorized by the owner (A1) to communicate with customers, AND
- Trained to this procedure (CS-0001) in their role file or by an Eco-directed task, AND
- Operating with a live product in scope.

Until the above are all true for a given agent: that agent may NOT send customer email, regardless of whether the email-send technical capability (T-0037) is enabled on their account.

At the time this policy is drafted, no agent meets all three conditions (CS-0001 is not yet A1-approved, no product is live). This section activates only when the hard gate in section 2 is fully open.

### 6.2 Approved channel and model

The only approved email send model at this time is the draft-into-owner-Gmail model:

1. The authorized CS agent composes a draft email. The draft must comply with sections 3, 4, and 5 of this policy.
2. The draft is placed into the owner's Gmail Drafts folder (not sent directly). No agent may trigger an email send autonomously.
3. The owner (jecki) reviews the draft and clicks Send. This is the per-action owner approval gate.
4. No email is sent to a customer without the owner completing step 3.

This model applies until the owner explicitly modifies it via a logged A1 decision.

### 6.3 Pre-send checklist (agent must verify before placing a draft)

- [ ] Customer name or "Dear Customer" address used correctly (section 4.1).
- [ ] Content falls within permitted scope (section 3.1). No prohibited content.
- [ ] No verbatim personal data included beyond what is necessary for the specific communication.
- [ ] No legal commitments, pricing, or roadmap detail included.
- [ ] Tone is warm, patient, plain (section 4.2).
- [ ] If the email references a product, the product is live and the information is from approved documentation.
- [ ] The agent has confirmed the send capability (T-0037 technical gate) is enabled on the account being used.

### 6.4 Hard limits

- No CS agent sends customer email autonomously. Owner-clicks-send is mandatory for every email.
- The APS-010 / design-partner hard limit stands: no agent contacts Adam (design partner) via email. That is owner-only relay. This policy does not change or soften that limit.
- No bulk email, marketing email, or acquisition outreach under this procedure. This procedure covers individual customer support and account communication only.

## 7. Escalation path

### 7.1 Rep to Mike

A CS rep must escalate to Mike when:
- The customer's issue is outside the rep's knowledge or authority.
- A customer is angry, distressed, or threatens to escalate.
- Any prohibited content (section 3.2) is implicated.
- A response would involve pricing, legal commitments, or roadmap detail.
- The rep is unsure whether a response is safe to send.

Loop cap: Mike gives the rep at most 2 rounds of guidance on a single case before deciding or escalating to Eco himself.

### 7.2 Mike to Eco

Mike escalates to Eco when:
- The customer issue requires a product, legal, or strategic decision beyond CS authority.
- A policy question arises that CS-0001 does not resolve.
- A CS rep has attempted prohibited customer contact (Mike holds and escalates immediately).
- An inbound customer contact arrives before the hard gate is open (hold and escalate, no response).

### 7.3 Eco to owner

Eco escalates to jecki (owner) when:
- The matter requires A1 (any customer communication constituting a legal commitment or company representation, per RL11 and constitution section 11).
- A new customer channel or tool is proposed (A1 required, gate first).
- Any CS matter the owner needs to decide.

### 7.4 Inbound contact before gates open

If a customer contacts the company before CS-0001 is A1-approved or before a product is live:
- The rep holds the contact. No response is sent.
- The rep escalates to Mike within the same cycle.
- Mike holds and escalates to Eco.
- Eco surfaces to owner if a response is needed.
- No agent improvises a response. Ever.

## 8. Privacy and compliance

- Israeli Privacy Protection Law (PPL) governs all customer data handling. No exceptions.
- Customer personal data is collected and used only for the specific stated purpose of the interaction.
- No customer personal data is stored verbatim in any tracked file (board, log, CS ticket summaries only).
- No customer data is shared with third-party tools or services without owner A1 and a gate review (Rambo security + Eyal legal).
- Any suspected data breach or privacy incident: Mike escalates to Eco immediately. Eco escalates to owner. The 72-hour PPL breach notification clock (AUD-002) is relevant; Eyal handles legal notification.
